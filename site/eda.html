Page 3 – Clustering and Anomaly Detection

Embedding source: CNN latent space & UMAP projections
Objectives: Group discovery and spectral validation

3.1 Clustering of Impedance Patterns

Natural groupings of electrochemical behaviour were identified by clustering both CNN latent embeddings and UMAP features. The following algorithms were benchmarked:

  • K-Means: Partitioning clusters based on Euclidean impedance similarity.
  • DBSCAN: Density-based clustering suitable for non-spherical manifolds.
  • Gaussian Mixture Models (GMM): Probabilistic modelling of overlapping impedance states.

Cluster validity was quantified using Silhouette, Davies–Bouldin, and Calinski–Harabasz indices. Distinct clusters corresponded to characteristic impedance signatures, including mid-frequency semicircle enlargement and low-frequency tail elongation, indicative of SEI growth and diffusion resistance increases.

3.2 Optimal Frequency Regions for SOC Prediction

Analysis of cluster centroids revealed that mid-frequency regions between 1–100 Hz delivered the greatest discriminative power for State-of-Charge (SOC) and State-of-Health (SOH) differentiation. These frequencies predominantly reflect charge-transfer resistance and interfacial kinetics, marking them as prime candidates for reduced-order modelling and real-time impedance-based diagnostics.

3.3 Anomaly Detection

Spectral integrity was safeguarded using a dual-stage anomaly detection scheme:

  • Isolation Forest: Detected spectra deviating from the learned impedance manifold.
  • Autoencoder Reconstruction Error: Flagged physically inconsistent curves by thresholding reconstruction loss.

Combining these strategies ensured that only electrochemically valid spectra contributed to downstream learning tasks and that aberrant measurements were escalated for manual review.
