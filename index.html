Introduction

This project focuses on identifying anomalous battery cells within groups of cells that share similar chemistries and mass loading. Using electrochemical impedance spectroscopy (EIS) and rate capability tests, the goal is to flag cells that are likely to underperform or fail before they enter later stages of testing or pack integration.

In particular, we are interested in:

  • How EIS features at different states of charge (SOC) relate to rate capability.
  • Which SOC windows are most predictive of poor performance.
  • How to define and detect "anomalous" cells within a batch.
